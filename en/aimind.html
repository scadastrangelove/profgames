What this layer is for

  • AI-Mind codes human-side outcomes: distorted belief, parasocial attachment, sycophancy amplification, AI-as-therapist, minors, self-harm cases, social displacement and protective use.
  • psAIcho is the model-side lens: sycophancy, persona instability, scheming/deception, anthropomorphic self-presentation, toxic mirroring and model-welfare as an open question.
  • HB01-HB06 are not new diagnoses. They are pre-AI human mechanisms - CASA, anthropomorphism, parasociality, ELIZA effect, online disinhibition and the Proteus effect - that AI can amplify.
  • The page below is a compact English guide. The full rendered catalogue and audit trail remain in the canonical Russian HTML and machine-readable artifacts.

Boundary patterns: AI denial, value refusal and governance activism

These are boundary patterns, not new clinical phenomena. They describe cultural reactions to AI uncertainty and should not be merged into a new PH16.

BP01

Folk AI detection / provenance panic

When provenance infrastructure is missing, communities replace argument checking with surface cues: em dashes, the Russian letter yo, smooth style, too-even structure or suspicious punctuation.

Guardrail: this is not proof that a specific text was written by AI. Rational source-checking is hygiene; the symptom starts when argument review becomes sign-counting.

BP02

Principled AI refusal / pro-human position

A value-based refusal to use AI as a defense of human authorship, labor identity, artistic practice or dataset ethics. It is not folk detection; it is a claim about who should create.

Guardrail: do not pathologize ethical refusal. Data consent, labor, copyright and environmental concerns can be legitimate positions.

HB02PH13PH15
BP03

Anti-development activism / Stop AI

Political action against advanced-AI development because of x-risk or governance risk: protests, hunger strikes and calls to pause the race toward more capable systems.

Guardrail: this is a governance reference, not a PH/HB/AB signal. It belongs closer to policy and risk-governance analysis than to individual psychological impact.

Evidence and claims

Evidence scale

A/B/C/D, shared with ProfGames

A = primary administrative, court, regulatory or peer-reviewed evidence. B = strong observational or major reporting. C = corporate narrative or practitioner literature. D = testimony, opinion or forecast.

What not to claim

No prevalence shortcut

Case pools, lawsuits and support-group reports are important signals, but they are not epidemiology. Use them as documented narratives unless a study gives a sampling frame.

psAIcho guardrail

Model behavior is not model psyche

AB01-AB06 describe functional behavior or dispositions. AB07 model welfare is an open question, not a claim that current models are conscious.

Machine-readable artifacts

Field names are already English and IDs are stable. The English reader layer deliberately links to the canonical artifacts instead of forking the dataset.
